# Accessibility at Scale in Product Teams

A practical playbook for making accessibility measurable and sustainable in fast-moving teams.

## Why Accessibility Gets Deprioritised

Most teams agree accessibility matters — but it still falls off the sprint board. The problem is that a11y is treated as a quality attribute rather than a feature.

## Making It Measurable

You can't improve what you don't measure. Start with:

1. **Automated audits** — axe-core in CI catches ~40% of issues
2. **Manual testing** — keyboard navigation, screen reader walkthroughs
3. **Scoring dashboards** — track Lighthouse a11y scores per route over time

## Building It Into the Process

- Add a11y acceptance criteria to every user story
- Include screen reader testing in your QA checklist
- Run quarterly audits with real assistive technology users

## The Component Library Advantage

> If your design system components are accessible, every product built on top inherits that accessibility for free.

Invest in accessible primitives: focus management, ARIA patterns, colour contrast tokens.

## Conclusion

Accessibility at scale is not about heroic individual effort — it's about systems, automation, and culture.
